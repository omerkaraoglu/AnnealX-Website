About readark

readark is an engineering company building intelligent embedded systems — products where firmware, hardware, and the user experience ship as a single coherent thing, not three teams compromising.

01 Why we exist

To build the products we'd want to own.

readark doesn't take outside design work. We pick problems we want solved — problems where a well-engineered embedded product would make somebody's day measurably better — and we build the solution under our own brand.

It is a deliberately narrow charter. We are not a consultancy. We are not chasing the trend cycle. We are building a small portfolio of products, each one of which has to earn its place by being genuinely better than what is already on the shelf.

02 What we work on

Domain-agnostic by market, focused by discipline.

Our products span sectors that on the surface have nothing to do with one another. The common thread is the engineering underneath: a microcontroller running a real-time control loop, sensors and actuators that have to behave in the real world, a user-facing interface that hides the complexity, and a firmware/UX team that owns the whole stack.

03 How we engineer

Four rules we don't trade away.

  • 01
    Plug in. It works. No accounts, no required app, no cloud login between you and the device you bought. Wireless features are optional and additive. If the network is down, the product is not.
  • 02
    Robustness before features. We would rather ship fewer features that never fail than a long datasheet that mostly works. Every feature in a readark product has to survive being used by somebody who has not read the manual.
  • 03
    Every claim is sourced. Specs, tolerances, material profiles, lifetime numbers — if it is on our page or in our manual, it is traceable to a measurement, a datasheet, or a peer-reviewed paper. Where the literature is thin, we say so.
  • 04
    Engineering writes the manual. The people who designed the firmware, the board, and the enclosure are the same people who write the user manual and the spec page. There is no marketing layer in between paraphrasing decisions for you.
